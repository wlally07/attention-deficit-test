About the ADHD Assessment

What is an ADHD assessment? +

An ADHD assessment is a comprehensive clinical process used by healthcare professionals to determine whether someone meets the criteria for Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder. There is no single exam or brain scan that diagnoses ADHD. Instead, a specialist evaluates a combination of symptoms, medical history, behaviour patterns, and daily functioning across different areas of life.

  • ADHD assessment is a multi-step clinical evaluation, not a single test
  • Specialists examine symptoms, history, and daily functioning
  • Results include a diagnosis and recommendations for support or treatment
Is the online assessment a real diagnosis? +

No online assessment can replace a formal clinical diagnosis. Our assessment is based on DSM-5 criteria and the WHO ASRS v1.1 scale. A positive result is a strong indicator that a full professional assessment is warranted, and that is exactly what our consultation service provides.

How long does the ADHD assessment take? +

The assessment takes approximately 5 to 8 minutes to complete. You will receive instant results on-screen along with a personalised symptom summary and a recommendation on whether a professional consultation would be beneficial.

What symptoms suggest I might have ADHD? +

ADHD symptoms generally fall into three main categories: inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity. Common signs include forgetfulness, distractibility, poor organisation, restlessness, fidgeting, and difficulty controlling impulses. Symptoms must interfere with daily functioning to indicate possible ADHD.

Adults & Diagnosis

Can adults be tested for ADHD? +

Yes, adults can absolutely be tested for ADHD. Although ADHD is often associated with childhood, many individuals are not diagnosed until adulthood. Our service offers professional consultations specifically designed for adults, available in over 40 countries.

Is ADHD different in adults compared to children? +

ADHD involves the same core symptoms in both children and adults, but the way these symptoms appear often changes with age. In children, ADHD tends to be more visible with obvious hyperactivity. In adults, it often manifests as inner restlessness, chronic procrastination, and difficulty managing time and responsibilities.

Can ADHD develop later in life? +

ADHD is considered a neurodevelopmental condition, meaning symptoms typically begin in childhood, even if they are not diagnosed until later. Most diagnostic guidelines require evidence that symptoms were present before age 12. However, many people are only identified in adulthood because their symptoms were previously overlooked.

Is ADHD the same as ADD? +

ADD and ADHD refer to the same underlying condition, but the terminology has changed over time. Today, ADHD is the official medical term, covering three distinct presentations: inattentive, hyperactive-impulsive, and combined.
