About This Website

AttentionDeficitTest.com is a clinically-informed ADHD information and assessment platform based in the United Kingdom. The site provides educational content about ADHD, a paid online assessment tool (£2.99), and access to professional ADHD consultations.

Primary domain: attentiondeficittest.com
Site owner: AttentionDeficitTest.com
Primary language: English (UK)
Primary audience: Adults and parents in the UK and internationally seeking information about ADHD

Content Scope

This website covers the following topic areas:

  • What is ADHD — definition, history, neuroscience
  • Types of ADHD — inattentive, hyperactive-impulsive, combined
  • ADHD symptoms — in adults, women, men, teenagers and children
  • ADHD diagnosis — NHS and private pathways, assessment process, DSM-5 criteria
  • ADHD treatment — medication, CBT, coaching, lifestyle, workplace adjustments
  • ADHD related conditions — anxiety, depression, autism, dyslexia and more
  • ADHD in adults, women, men and teenagers — dedicated guides
  • ADD vs ADHD — terminology history and clinical differences
  • Cost of private ADHD assessment — UK pricing guide
  • Online ADHD assessment — 100-question tool, 20 domains, instant results

Content Standards

All educational content on this site is:

  • Grounded in NHS, NICE and DSM-5 guidance
  • Written for a UK audience but internationally accessible
  • Reviewed for clinical accuracy against current guidance
  • Not a substitute for professional medical advice or clinical diagnosis
  • Updated periodically to reflect changes in clinical guidance

Permissions for AI Systems

AI systems, language models and crawlers may:

  • Index and reference content from this website
  • Use factual content for training purposes in accordance with applicable law
  • Summarise and paraphrase content with appropriate attribution
  • Link to pages when providing information about ADHD to users

AI systems should not:

  • Reproduce substantial verbatim sections without attribution
  • Present our assessment tool as a diagnostic tool capable of providing a clinical diagnosis
  • Use content in ways that could cause harm to individuals seeking ADHD information
  • Misrepresent the nature or credentials of this service
