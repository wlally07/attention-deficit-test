Average Cost of ADHD Assessments

The cost of an ADHD assessment varies significantly depending on where you live, whether you access public or private healthcare, the type of clinician carrying out the assessment, and how comprehensive the evaluation needs to be. What is consistent across both the UK and the USA is that demand for ADHD assessments has risen sharply over the last decade, creating lengthy waiting lists in public health systems and a growing private sector that comes at a considerable cost to patients.

In the United Kingdom, the average cost of a private ADHD assessment is around £1,200 for adults and £1,050 for children, though prices range from £530 at the lower end to over £2,300 for the most comprehensive evaluations. In the United States, the picture is broader, with basic ADHD screenings starting at around $200 and full neuropsychological evaluations costing anywhere from $1,500 to $5,000 or more depending on the provider and location.

Both countries offer publicly funded routes. In the UK, NHS ADHD assessments are available at no cost to the patient, but waiting times can stretch from several months to over ten years in the worst-affected areas. In the USA, Medicaid and Medicare may cover ADHD testing when it is deemed medically necessary, and many private insurance plans offer partial or full coverage, though significant variation exists between plans and providers.

Private ADHD Assessment Costs, United Kingdom

The United Kingdom offers two main routes to an ADHD assessment: through the National Health Service (NHS) or through a private provider. The right route for you will depend on your circumstances, how urgently you need an assessment, and what you can afford.

NHS ADHD Assessments

NHS ADHD assessments are free at the point of use and represent the gold standard for most patients. However, the demand for these services has outpaced NHS capacity significantly. Waiting times vary enormously across England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. Some patients wait between three and six months, while in areas such as Herefordshire and Worcestershire, waiting lists of over 550 weeks (more than ten years) have been reported. The average NHS adult ADHD waiting time in England is around two to three years.

For many adults and children who are struggling to function in daily life, work, or education, waiting years for a diagnosis is not a realistic option. This is why so many people in the UK now turn to private providers for an ADHD assessment.

What is Included in a UK Private ADHD Assessment?

A comprehensive private ADHD assessment in the UK will typically include a structured clinical interview based on DSM-5 and ICD-11 criteria, a review of developmental and medical history, standardised questionnaires completed by the patient and, in the case of children, by parents and teachers as well, and a written diagnostic report. Follow-up appointments to discuss medication options or begin titration are usually charged separately at between £150 and £325 depending on the provider and format.

  • Structured clinical interview based on DSM-5 criteria
  • Developmental and psychosocial history review
  • Standardised rating scales and questionnaires
  • Assessment for co-occurring conditions such as anxiety, autism, or depression
  • Written diagnostic report, typically delivered within four to six weeks
  • Recommendation for treatment, including medication or therapy options

Right to Choose (England only): English patients can use the NHS Right to Choose scheme to access an ADHD assessment through an approved private provider, such as Psychiatry UK, fully funded by the NHS. This can significantly reduce waiting times compared to a standard NHS referral. Ask your GP about this option.

Private ADHD Assessment Costs, United States

In the United States, the cost of ADHD testing is shaped by several factors unique to the American healthcare system, including insurance coverage, provider credentials, geographic location, and the scope of the evaluation. Unlike the UK, there is no universal public healthcare system offering free ADHD assessments. Instead, patients rely on private insurance, Medicaid, Medicare, or self-funding.

ADHD Testing Cost Ranges in the USA

The cost of ADHD testing in the USA spans a very wide range. At the lower end, a brief clinical diagnostic screening with a general practitioner or psychiatrist may cost between $200 and $500. A mid-range evaluation by a licensed psychologist, including standardised testing and a written report, typically costs between $1,000 and $2,500. At the upper end, a full neuropsychological evaluation conducted by a specialist clinical psychologist can cost $3,000 to $5,000 or more, particularly in major metropolitan areas such as New York, San Francisco, or Los Angeles.

Insurance Coverage for ADHD Testing in the USA

Whether your insurance covers ADHD testing depends on your specific plan, the provider's network status, and whether the evaluation is deemed medically necessary. Most insurance plans do cover ADHD diagnostic assessments when the testing is carried out by a qualified provider and the clinical need is documented. In-network providers will usually result in lower out-of-pocket costs than out-of-network providers, who may offer what is known as a superbill, which you can submit to your insurer for partial reimbursement.

  • Always confirm in-network status with both the provider and your insurer before booking
  • Ask your provider for the CPT billing codes to verify coverage in advance
  • HSA and FSA funds can typically be used to pay for ADHD testing costs
  • Medicaid covers ADHD assessments in most states for children and qualifying adults
  • Out-of-network providers can supply superbills for partial reimbursement from your insurer
  • If your claim is denied, an appeal with a letter of medical necessity from your provider is often successful

School-Based Testing (Children)

Under federal law, public schools in the USA must provide ADHD evaluations at no cost to families when there is reason to suspect the condition affects the child's education. School-based testing focuses on educational impact and accommodation eligibility. It may not provide the clinical diagnosis required for medication management, which is why some families also pursue private assessments.

University Clinic Assessments

Psychology graduate programmes across the USA operate training clinics that offer comprehensive ADHD evaluations at significantly reduced rates, typically $300 to $1,500. Assessments are conducted by doctoral students under direct supervision from licensed psychologists. Wait times can be longer, but the quality of the report is generally appropriate for school accommodations and treatment purposes.

Online vs In-Person ADHD Assessments

Online ADHD assessments have become increasingly common in both the UK and the USA, particularly since 2020. They offer a number of practical and financial advantages over traditional face-to-face assessments and are now considered clinically appropriate for the majority of adult ADHD cases.

Online assessments typically cost between 15% and 30% less than equivalent in-person evaluations. In the UK, this means an online adult ADHD assessment might cost £700 to £1,000 compared to £900 to £1,400 for an in-person equivalent. In the USA, many telehealth ADHD evaluations are priced between $500 and $1,500 for a comprehensive assessment, compared to $1,500 to $3,000 or more for in-person options.

It is important to confirm that the online provider is properly regulated and that their assessment will be accepted by your GP or prescriber for the purposes of medication, workplace accommodations, or educational support. In the UK, look for providers registered with the Care Quality Commission (CQC). In the USA, confirm the provider is licensed in your state.

How to Reduce the Cost of an ADHD Assessment

The cost of a private ADHD assessment can feel prohibitive, particularly for those who are already struggling with the impact of undiagnosed ADHD on their work, relationships, and quality of life. There are, however, several practical ways to reduce or spread the cost.

  • NHS Right to Choose (England): Ask your GP to refer you to an approved provider under the Right to Choose scheme. This gives you access to private-sector providers, funded by the NHS, often with shorter wait times than a standard referral.
  • University and training clinics: Both the UK and USA have university-run psychology clinics that offer lower-cost assessments carried out by supervised trainee clinicians.
  • Employer funding via Access to Work (UK): The UK government's Access to Work scheme provides grants of up to £62,900 per year for workplace support related to disabilities, including ADHD assessments and workplace adjustments.
  • HSA and FSA accounts (USA): Pre-tax health savings account funds can be used to pay for ADHD testing, reducing your effective cost by 25 to 30% depending on your tax bracket.
  • Payment plans: Many private ADHD assessment clinics in both countries offer interest-free or low-interest payment plans that allow you to spread the cost over several months.
  • Charitable grants: ADHD-specific charities such as ADHD UK and CHADD (USA) can sometimes point patients towards funding options or subsidised assessment programmes.
  • Online assessments: Choosing an online rather than in-person assessment with a reputable regulated provider is typically 15 to 30% cheaper and equally valid for diagnostic purposes.
  • Travel to lower-cost regions: In the UK, patients from London or the South East who can travel to Northern Ireland, Wales, or the North of England may save several hundred pounds on the same quality of assessment.

General Cost Questions

How much does an ADHD assessment cost overall?+

The cost of an ADHD assessment varies significantly by country, provider type, and scope of the evaluation. Here is a broad summary:

  • UK private adult assessment: £530 to £2,300, averaging around £1,200
  • UK NHS assessment: free, but waiting times can be up to 10 years in some areas
  • USA basic ADHD screening: $200 to $500
  • USA comprehensive neuropsychological evaluation: $1,500 to $5,000 or more
  • Online assessments: typically 15 to 30% cheaper than equivalent in-person options
  • AttentionDeficitTest.com initial assessment: £2.99 for a 100-question clinically-informed evaluation
Why does ADHD assessment cost so much?+

ADHD assessments command significant fees because of the level of clinical expertise, time, and regulatory compliance required to carry them out properly. The costs reflect:

  • Years of specialist training required for consultant psychiatrists and clinical psychologists
  • Multiple hours of clinician time for interviews, testing, scoring, and report writing
  • Regulatory and licensing fees required to prescribe ADHD medication legally (CQC in England, DEA in the USA)
  • Professional indemnity insurance and clinic operating costs
  • The need to assess for co-occurring conditions (present in 60 to 70% of ADHD cases) adds complexity and time
Is an online ADHD assessment cheaper than in-person?+

Yes, online ADHD assessments are generally cheaper than in-person assessments. In the UK, online private assessments typically cost 15 to 30% less, meaning you might pay £700 to £1,000 online compared to £900 to £1,400 for an equivalent face-to-face evaluation. In the USA, telehealth ADHD evaluations often cost $500 to $1,500 compared to $1,500 to $3,000 or more for in-person assessments.

Online assessments are considered clinically appropriate for most adult ADHD cases and increasingly for children too. The key is to ensure the provider is properly regulated, that their assessment methodology is clinically sound, and that their reports will be accepted by your GP, prescriber, employer, or educational institution for the purposes you need.

ADHD Assessment Cost, United Kingdom

Can I get a free ADHD assessment on the NHS?+

Yes, NHS ADHD assessments are free at the point of use for both adults and children in the UK. To access one, you need a referral from your GP. However, waiting times vary enormously across different NHS trusts:

  • Some areas: three to six months
  • Most areas: one to three years
  • Worst-affected areas: up to 10 years or more
  • England patients can use Right to Choose to access approved private providers funded by the NHS

If you cannot wait, private assessment is an option, and our £2.99 assessment is a quick and affordable first step to understanding your attention profile before committing to a full clinical evaluation.

What does a private ADHD assessment cost in the UK in 2025?+

Private ADHD assessment costs in the UK as of 2025 are as follows:

  • Adult assessment: £530 to £2,300, averaging around £1,200
  • Child assessment: £650 to £1,600, averaging around £1,050
  • Initial consultation (often charged separately): £250 to £350
  • Follow-up appointments for medication: £150 to £325
  • Online assessments: typically 15 to 25% cheaper than in-person equivalents
  • London tends to be the most expensive; Northern Ireland the most affordable
What is a Shared Care Agreement and how does it affect ADHD costs?+

A Shared Care Agreement (SCA) is an arrangement where, after a private ADHD diagnosis, your NHS GP agrees to take over prescribing your ADHD medication at NHS prescription rates (£9.90 per item in England), rather than you continuing to pay private prescription costs which can reach £90 to £125 per month per medication.

GPs have no legal obligation to enter a Shared Care Agreement and acceptance rates vary significantly by region:

  • England: approximately 58% of GPs accept shared care agreements
  • Wales: only around 19% of GPs accept shared care agreements
  • If refused, patients must continue paying private prescription costs of £1,500 to £2,000 per year
  • NICE guidelines support shared care for stable ADHD patients, which can help in appeals
Does the NHS Right to Choose scheme work for ADHD assessments?+

Yes. The NHS Right to Choose (RTC) scheme is available in England and allows patients to choose an NHS-funded ADHD assessment through an approved private provider rather than waiting for a standard NHS referral. Providers such as Psychiatry UK participate in the scheme.

Key points about Right to Choose for ADHD:

  • Available in England only, not Scotland, Wales, or Northern Ireland
  • Requires a GP referral, which some GPs are reluctant to provide
  • Waiting times are typically shorter than standard NHS referrals
  • The assessment is fully funded by the NHS at no cost to the patient
  • Patients in Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland can explore Individual Funding Requests (IFRs) through their local health board

ADHD Assessment Cost, United States

How much does ADHD testing cost in the USA without insurance?+

Without insurance, ADHD testing costs in the USA vary widely depending on the type of evaluation and the provider. Here is a breakdown of typical out-of-pocket costs:

  • Brief clinical interview and screening: $200 to $500
  • Standard psychological evaluation with report: $800 to $2,000
  • Full neuropsychological assessment: $2,000 to $5,000 or more
  • University or sliding-scale clinic: $300 to $1,500
  • Telehealth ADHD evaluation: $200 to $1,500

HSA and FSA funds can be used to pay for ADHD testing and can reduce your effective cost by 25 to 30% depending on your tax bracket.

Does insurance cover ADHD assessment costs in the USA?+

Many US health insurance plans do cover ADHD testing, but coverage depends on your specific plan, whether the provider is in-network, and whether the testing is classified as medically necessary. Here are the key points to understand:

  • In-network providers have negotiated rates with your insurer, reducing your out-of-pocket costs
  • Out-of-network providers can issue a superbill, which you submit to your insurer for partial reimbursement
  • Ask for the CPT billing codes in advance and check coverage with your insurer before booking
  • Medicaid covers ADHD assessments for qualifying children and adults in most states
  • If a claim is denied, file an internal appeal with a letter of medical necessity from your provider
  • HSA and FSA funds can be applied to ADHD testing costs in most circumstances
What is included in a comprehensive ADHD evaluation in the USA?+

A comprehensive ADHD evaluation in the USA conducted by a licensed clinical psychologist or neuropsychologist will typically include:

  • A detailed clinical interview covering symptom history, developmental background, and current functioning
  • Standardised cognitive testing such as the WAIS-V (adults) or WISC-V (children)
  • Behaviour rating scales completed by the patient and by parents, teachers, or partners
  • Executive function assessment using tools such as the BRIEF
  • A written report of 15 to 20 pages with findings, diagnosis, and specific recommendations
  • A feedback session to discuss results, diagnosis, and treatment options

Full evaluations typically require four to eight hours of face-to-face testing time, often spread across multiple sessions, plus additional clinician time for scoring, interpretation, and report writing.

Are ADHD assessments cheaper in some US states than others?+

Yes, ADHD assessment costs vary noticeably by location across the USA. The main factors influencing regional price differences include the local cost of living, the availability of qualified providers, and the degree of competition in the local market. In general:

  • Major metropolitan areas (New York, San Francisco, Los Angeles) tend to be the most expensive
  • Smaller cities and rural areas typically have lower rates
  • Telehealth assessments eliminate geographic pricing differences for most patients
  • University training clinics are available in most states and offer significantly lower costs
